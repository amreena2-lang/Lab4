Undergraduate Program (B.S. in IS)

Our Information Systems program teaches students to analyze, design, and manage data-driven systems that help organizations operate efficiently and make informed decisions. Students gain hands-on experience with modern technologies, project management, and systems development.

Career Opportunities

Graduates from UMBC’s IS program are highly sought after in the technology, business, and government sectors. Below are some of the career paths our students pursue:

Business Analyst: Bridge the gap between technology and business needs by analyzing workflows, gathering requirements, and proposing efficient system solutions that improve organizational performance.
Data Analyst: Transform raw data into meaningful insights. Data analysts use tools like SQL, Python, and visualization software to guide data-driven decision-making across industries.
Web Developer: Design and build dynamic websites and web applications that enhance user experiences. This role combines creativity with programming skills in HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and backend frameworks.
Cybersecurity Specialist: Protect organizations from digital threats by managing security protocols, identifying vulnerabilities, and developing strategies to safeguard information systems.
Systems Analyst: Evaluate existing systems and design new ones that optimize business processes. Systems analysts act as problem-solvers who align IT capabilities with organizational goals.

UMBC IS graduates work at companies such as Northrop Grumman, T. Rowe Price, Amazon, Deloitte, and various federal agencies, continuing UMBC’s tradition of excellence in tech innovation and service.
